school The Ultimate Junior Financial Literacy Guide

Chapter 1: Income vs. Expenses

In the financial world, money only moves in two directions: In (Income) and Out (Expenses).

  • Active Income: Money you work for directly (like vacuuming upstairs).
  • Passive Income: Money that works for you (like the interest paid by a bank savings account).
  • Expenses: Any money you spend to buy items (like card booster packs).

Chapter 2: Needs vs. Wants

Before any purchase, filter the item: Needs are absolute requirements to stay healthy (healthy food, safety). Wants are items that are fun, but you can live comfortably without (video games, soccer cards).

Chapter 3: The 50/50 Blueprint

By splitting every single dollar in half, you build a lifetime safety cushion while still allowing yourself to buy and enjoy smaller immediate goals.

local_post_office The Meantime Envelope System

Since you don't have a bank card in your hands yet, find two separate paper envelopes (or glass jars) and label them:

Envelope A: Spend (50%)

Carry this cash with you when going to the store for soccer cards or movies.

Envelope B: Savings (50%)

Keep this envelope tucked safely away in your room. This money is locked!

🔄 The Bank Deposit Trick

Once your physical Savings Envelope reaches a milestone (like $10 or $20), hand it to your dad and ask him to deposit it into your actual account. This proves your financial maturity instantly!

view_sidebar One Bank Account: The Single-Drawer Trick

If you only have one chequing account and NO secondary savings folder in your bank yet, you can use Ledger Separation.

Imagine your single bank account is a drawer, but your digital Chore Hub acts as a solid cardboard divider right down the middle. Even though the bank holds the total sum in one slot, your tracker keeps a strict record of what part is allowed to be spent, and what part is strictly locked away.

directions_run Morning Mobility & Core Activation

Mobility wakes up your muscles and keeps you flexible so you don't get hurt. Core activation fires up your abs and hips so you have more balance when shield-dribbling. Perform these simple movements:

🧘 1. Spine Waker (Cat-Cow Stretch)

Get on hands and knees. Arch your back up like a cat, then arch it down while looking up. Do this slowly 5 times.

🐜 2. Core Balancing deadbugs

Lie flat on your back with legs and arms straight up in the air. Slowly lower your right arm and left leg to the floor, bring them back, and swap. Do 5 reps each side.

🪵 3. Abdominals Activation (The Plank)

Hold a standard elbow plank for 30 to 45 seconds. Squeeze your stomach tight and keep your body perfectly straight like a soccer field line.

psychology Daily Visual Scanning Workout

Elite midfielders like Kevin De Bruyne scan their surroundings every 2 seconds. This drill trains your eyes to spot teammates and opponents before receiving the soccer ball.

🎯 Wall Dribble & Glance (5-8 Mins)

Pass a soccer ball against a wall in your basement or backyard. Right before you receive it back, quickly snap your head to look behind your shoulder, then turn and shield the ball.

👀 Rapid Tracking

Sit upright. Without moving your head, look all the way up, down, left, then right. Do this in circles for 1 minute to stretch and warm up your eye muscles.
